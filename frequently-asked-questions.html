Frequently Asked Questions

Explore some of our most commonly asked questions below. If you don't find what you are looking for, you are welcome to contact us with your specific requirements.

Sourcing and Supply

How do your sourcing fees work?

We offer flexible engagement models: Client-Managed (we provide on-ground support while you manage supplier directly), PSG-Managed (we handle end-to-end from vetting to delivery), or Hybrid. Fees may be structured as a fixed project fee, hourly rate, percentage of order value, or commission.

Is there a minimum order value for sourcing projects?

For sourcing and supply projects, our usual minimum is around USD $10,000 / NZD $15,000 net monthly order value. This can vary depending on product complexity, workload, and current capacity.

Why do you have a minimum order value?

Sourcing from China takes experience, coordination, and time. A single USD $10,000 order is usually far more efficient to manage properly than ten separate USD $1,000 orders. If your order is below our minimum, we may still help through paid consulting or freight support.

Are there any products you do not supply?

We do not source counterfeit, copyright-infringing, or otherwise illegal products. As the importer, you are responsible for ensuring compliance with your local laws and import regulations.

Can you supply and ship goods worldwide?

We can ship to any country not subject to trade restrictions under WTO, UN, or international law. Buyers are responsible for ensuring their destination is not under any active trade embargo.

Do you only source within Guangdong?

No, we source from anywhere in China. While Guangdong projects often involve lower travel costs, we work with trusted partners nationwide to manage projects efficiently.

Freight & Warehousing

Can I use your freight service without using your sourcing service?

Yes, we offer freight services to all customers. Sourcing clients receive preferential rates, but non-sourcing customers are welcome to request quotes. Minimum shipment volumes may apply depending on destination.

Do you ship from China to New Zealand and Australia?

Yes, we regularly handle freight to both NZ and Australia, including major centres and regional/rural addresses depending on the freight method.

What freight options do you offer?

Sea freight (LCL/FCL), air freight, courier, express shipping, door-to-door, warehouse consolidation, and Amazon FBA preparation support.

Can you collect goods from my supplier in China?

Yes. We can coordinate pickup from your supplier's address, warehouse, or factory in China. We'll need supplier contact details, pickup address, carton details, and product information.

Can you consolidate goods from multiple suppliers?

Yes. Consolidation helps reduce duplicated freight and customs charges. We'll check product types, sizes, supplier locations, and timing to determine suitability.

Do freight prices fluctuate?

Yes. Freight prices can change due to fuel costs, exchange rates, capacity, peak season, and port congestion. Any estimate is indicative until shipment details are confirmed.

What information do you need for a freight quote?

We typically need: pickup city, delivery address, INCOTERMS, product name/HSCODE, carton count/dimensions/weight, total value, and any special handling requirements (batteries, liquids, etc).

eCommerce Seller Services

Can you help if we are new to eCommerce?

Our full sourcing services are best for businesses with an active budget and product direction. If you're just starting, hourly consulting may be a better fit before committing to manufacturing.

Do you assist with dropshipping?

We source direct from China and don't operate as a dropshipping service. However, we can help established dropshippers transition toward holding inventory with store-and-ship operations.

Can you ship goods to Amazon FBA?

Yes, we can ship directly to Amazon FBA warehouses worldwide using Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) shipping options.

Can you print and apply product labels for FBA?

Yes. We can help arrange printing and application of FNSKU labels, carton labels, and barcodes. Clients must provide the correct label files and Amazon shipment requirements before packing.
